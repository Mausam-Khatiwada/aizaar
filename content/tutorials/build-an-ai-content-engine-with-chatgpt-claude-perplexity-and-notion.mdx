## QuickAnswer
A real AI content engine is a process, not a prompt. Use Perplexity for source discovery, Claude/ChatGPT for drafting and transformation, and Notion for editorial control and publishing cadence.

## The engine architecture
- Input layer: market questions, product updates, customer pain points
- Research layer: source-backed insight extraction
- Draft layer: long-form, short-form, and platform variants
- QA layer: fact check, tone check, conversion intent check
- Distribution layer: publishing + repurposing

If one layer is weak, output quality collapses.

## Step 1: Build your topic backlog
Create a quarterly backlog in [Notion AI](/tools/notion-ai):
- comparisons
- alternatives
- tutorials
- case studies
- opinion/editorial pieces

Each topic should map to one business goal: traffic, trust, trial, or conversion.

## Step 2: Research with citation discipline
Use [Perplexity AI](/tools/perplexity-ai) to gather:
- source URLs
- recent product updates
- competitor positioning
- contradictory claims

Save links and claims together. Do not separate them.

## Step 3: Draft with model specialization
Use both [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt) and [Claude](/tools/claude):
- one for first draft
- one for critical rewrite and argument tightening

This dual-pass workflow reduces bland output and improves clarity.

## Step 4: Editorial QA gates
Before publishing, enforce:
1. source-backed factual claims
2. unique angle (not generic summary)
3. actionable takeaways
4. persona relevance
5. clear CTA alignment

If a draft fails 2+ gates, recycle it instead of forcing publish.

## Step 5: Repurpose without quality loss
From one long article, generate:
- 2 short LinkedIn posts
- 1 X thread
- 1 newsletter blurb
- 1 comparison card

Repurposing is where efficiency comes from, but keep human edits for voice consistency.

## Editorial cadence model
- Monday: research + outline
- Tuesday: long-form draft
- Wednesday: edit + QA
- Thursday: publish + repurpose
- Friday: metrics review + backlog updates

Keep the loop predictable. Consistency beats bursts.

## Metrics that actually matter
- publish consistency
- average time from brief to publish
- revision cycles per article
- CTR on high-intent pages
- assisted conversions from content

Avoid vanity metrics without business context.

## South Asian market adaptation
- maintain local examples and pricing context
- include mixed-language hooks where audience expects it
- account for region-specific platform behavior
- prioritize mobile readability and concise CTA copy

## FAQs
### Is one model enough for content operations?
It can work, but dual-model editing generally improves quality and reduces blind spots.

### How many articles per week should a small team target?
Start with 1-2 high-quality pieces plus repurposed derivatives.

### Can AI replace editors?
No. AI accelerates production, but editorial judgment creates trust.

### Should we publish daily for SEO?
Not unless quality is stable. Depth and consistency outperform low-quality volume.

### What should be automated first?
Outline generation, repurposing drafts, and status workflows.
