## QuickAnswer
For commercial-quality AI video, split the pipeline by capability: concept/style locking, scene generation, and voice/narration production. Do not rely on one tool for all three.

## Pipeline overview
- Visual ideation: [Midjourney](/tools/midjourney)
- Scene generation and editing: [Runway ML](/tools/runway-ml), [Sora](/tools/sora)
- Voice and narration: [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs)
- Final narrative pass: [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt) or [Claude](/tools/claude)

## Stage 1: Concept lock and style bible
Before generating scenes, define:
- color language
- camera movement style
- pacing profile
- narrative tone
- prohibited visual artifacts

This style bible prevents "new video, new style" inconsistency.

## Stage 2: Shot planning and prompt design
Break campaign into shots:
1. hook shot
2. credibility shot
3. product/benefit shot
4. CTA shot

Use short, deterministic prompts. Long prompts often introduce drift.

## Stage 3: Generation workflow
Use [Runway ML](/tools/runway-ml) for iterative control and [Sora](/tools/sora) for high-impact hero sequences where available.

Generation guidelines:
- render low-cost previews first
- approve motion and framing
- upscale only approved shots
- maintain naming conventions for shot versions

## Stage 4: Voice and audio system
Use [ElevenLabs](/tools/elevenlabs) with structured script blocks:
- intro hook
- body explanation
- CTA close

Keep pacing markers in script. It reduces re-record loops.

## Stage 5: QA checklist before export
- lip sync or timing consistency
- brand-safe claims
- subtitle readability on mobile
- scene transition continuity
- CTA clarity in final 3 seconds

No campaign should ship without this pass.

## Stage 6: Delivery variants
Export master first, then derive:
- 9:16 short-form
- 1:1 social cut
- 16:9 landing page cut

Do not generate every aspect ratio from scratch; derive from approved master.

## Cost and time optimization
- batch similar shots together
- reuse prompt scaffolds
- maintain reusable voice presets
- archive successful projects as templates

These four habits improve margin more than model switching.

## South Asian campaign adaptation
- localize spoken examples and references
- ensure accents/intonation fit audience expectations
- include festival and regional commerce context where relevant
- validate visual cues for cultural fit, not only aesthetics

## FAQs
### Is this workflow suitable for solo creators?
Yes, but start with fewer shots and one channel-specific format.

### Which tool should lead the pipeline?
Runway for control-heavy iteration. Sora for select high-impact sequences.

### Can I skip voice cloning?
Yes. High-quality stock voices are often enough for many ad formats.

### How do I reduce visual inconsistency?
Lock style references before generation and avoid changing prompt structure mid-project.

### What is the biggest production risk?
Publishing without QA on claims and final CTA clarity.
