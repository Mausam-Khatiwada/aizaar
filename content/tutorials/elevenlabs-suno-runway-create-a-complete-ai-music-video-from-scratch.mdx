## QuickAnswer
Use ElevenLabs for vocal lines, Suno for music bed and structure, then Runway for visual scenes. Keep one creative brief across all three tools for consistent output.

## Pre-production brief
Define:
- theme and emotional arc
- visual style references
- tempo range and vocal tone
- final platform aspect ratio

## Step 1: Generate voice elements in ElevenLabs
Use [ElevenLabs](https://elevenlabs.io?ref=toolverse) for spoken intro/outro or stylistic vocal overlays. Export clean stems for later mix.

## Step 2: Build song in Suno
Use [Suno](https://suno.com?ref=toolverse) prompts with explicit structure:
`ambient pop, 98 BPM, emotional hook, atmospheric bridge, uplifting outro`

Generate 2-3 versions and pick the one with strongest chorus.

## Step 3: Create visuals in Runway
In [Runway ML](https://runwayml.com?ref=toolverse), generate scenes that match each song segment:
- intro = slower motion, lower contrast
- chorus = high energy, brighter palette
- bridge = abstract transitional visuals

## Step 4: Edit and sync
- Align cuts to beat markers.
- Match color grade across all scenes.
- Keep title and credits minimal.

## Step 5: Publish formats
- 16:9 for YouTube
- 9:16 for Shorts/Reels
- 1:1 teaser for social feed posts

## FAQs
### Can this workflow work without manual editing?
You still need manual sequencing and pacing decisions.

### Which tool is hardest to learn?
Runway usually has the biggest creative control learning curve.

### Do I need paid plans for all three?
For serious output quality and export control, yes.

### How long does one video take?
A polished first video often takes 3-6 hours.

### Is this usable for client work?
Yes, with clear licensing checks and brand-safe review.
