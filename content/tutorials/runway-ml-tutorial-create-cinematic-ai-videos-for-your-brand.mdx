## QuickAnswer
For brand-safe results in Runway, lock style early and keep scene prompts consistent. Most quality issues come from prompt drift between shots.

## Production flow
1. Define a mini storyboard with 4-6 shots.
2. Generate style reference frames.
3. Use [Runway ML](https://runwayml.com?ref=toolverse) text-to-video or image-to-video.
4. Refine motion with Motion Brush.
5. Export in platform-native format.

## Prompt framework
`Shot type + subject + movement + mood + lighting + lens language`

Example:
`Close-up product reveal, slow dolly-in, moody teal-orange lighting, cinematic depth of field`

## Gen-3 style consistency tips
- Reuse key subject descriptors.
- Keep lens and lighting cues stable.
- Iterate one scene at a time.

## Export settings that matter
- 1080x1920 for reels/shorts.
- 1920x1080 for YouTube.
- Keep bitrate high for post-edit flexibility.

## FAQs
### Is Runway good for ad creatives?
Yes, especially early concept and fast visual testing.

### Should I use text-to-video or image-to-video first?
Image-to-video is usually more controllable for brand work.

### What is Motion Brush useful for?
Adding intentional localized motion to static elements.

### Can beginners use Gen-3 features?
Yes, but start with short clips and simple prompts.

### Is Runway better than Pika for cinematic control?
Many teams prefer Runway for control depth.
