## QuickAnswer
Use a short script, choose a stable voice preset, and export at high bitrate. Do not start with voice cloning before you learn pacing and pronunciation controls.

## Step 1: Set up your workspace
1. Create an account at [ElevenLabs](https://elevenlabs.io?ref=toolverse).
2. Pick one base voice that matches your client type.
3. Create folders by client and language so your files stay organized.

## Step 2: Generate your first voiceover
- Paste a short script (60-120 words).
- Use punctuation for pacing.
- Generate 2-3 takes and compare tone.

Prompting tip:
`Friendly tutorial tone, clear pronunciation, medium pace, slight enthusiasm`

## Step 3: Export like a pro
- Use high-quality MP3 or WAV.
- Normalize volume in your editor.
- Deliver two variants: natural and slightly energetic.

## Voice cloning basics
Voice cloning works best with clean dry audio samples. For freelancers, only use cloning with explicit client approval and legal ownership of source voice.

## Freelance use cases
- YouTube intros
- Explainer videos
- Product demos
- E-learning narrations

## FAQs
### Is ElevenLabs good for beginners?
Yes, especially if you stick to presets before advanced cloning.

### Which export format should I use?
WAV for editing workflows, MP3 for quick delivery.

### Can I use it for client ads?
Yes, check plan and usage rights before publishing.

### How many takes should I generate?
At least 2-3 to compare pacing and tone.

### Is voice cloning required?
No, most freelancers get great results with stock voices.
